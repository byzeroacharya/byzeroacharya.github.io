JUDGEMENT CRITERIA

  • Theme interpretation, creativity and innovation - 40%
  • Choreography and coordination – 40%
  • Costumes and Props – 20%

GUIDELINES

  • The performance time for each participating team is 5 minutes and a maximum of 3 minutes for stage set up, narration and clearance.
  • Minimum of 8 dancers
  • Maximum of 12 with 6 present on stage at all points of time
  • Narration is a must and should be included in these 8 minutes
  • Vulgarity will lead to disqualification
  • Usage of props is compulsory and delegates will not be allowed to participate for safety reasons if dangerous prop work and stunts are used.

RULES AND REGULATIONS:

  • Vulgarity and any of offensive behaviour will result in immediate disqualification.
  • Decision of the Jury is final.
  • 10 % of the points will be deducted for every minute after exceeding the given time limit.
  • Abusive or offensive language in the music lyrics will lead to disqualification.
  • Discrimination against any race, language, religion or person will lead to disqualification.

STAGE LAYOUT AND SCREENING PROCESS:

Each team is required to send in the following

  • A YouTube link of your hidden HD quality video, with a minimum run time of 2.5 minutes, must be submitted to the following Email address – zonasa2016.dance@gmail.com by 3pm on 14th September ’16 and the video with college code should be made private on YouTube.
  • The video should include the narration of your inferred synopsis from the brief within the given run time.
  • Performers must be dressed presentably in the screening video, not necessarily in their final costumes.
  • A PDF file of all the costume design and prop work sketches need to be sent along with the video to the Email address mentioned.

The final date for registration is 7th September 2016.

SCREENING CRITERIA:

  • Choreography.
  • Theme interpretation.
  • Design of costumes and prop work.

Please note: Any discrepancy between the final show and the screening video will result in the college being ineligible to compete.

The music used in the screening video need not be the music for the final performance.
